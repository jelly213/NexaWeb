Traceable from goat to garment

Cashmere worth keeping for a lifetime.

Our fibre is combed by hand each spring from goats raised on the Alashan plateau, then sorted for length and fineness — only the longest, finest 15.5-micron strands make the cut. Longer fibres mean fewer pills and a garment that softens rather than wears.

We knit in small batches with a family-run mill we've partnered with for a decade, so every seam is linked, not overlocked. It costs more. It also lasts a decade longer.
